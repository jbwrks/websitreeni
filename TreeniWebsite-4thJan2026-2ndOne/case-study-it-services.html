Case Study: Building a Supplier Sustainability Program

How a leading IT services provider gained visibility into 2,000+ suppliers

The Challenge

As a leading global IT services provider, the client had committed to ambitious sustainability targets, including net-zero emissions by 2040. However, over 60% of their carbon footprint came from their supply chain (Scope 3).

The procurement team worked with over 2,000 suppliers but had virtually no visibility into their ESG performance. They couldn't answer basic questions: Which suppliers had climate commitments? Who was at risk of non-compliance?

Without a credible program, they risked losing competitive advantage in European markets where customers were demanding this data.

The Solution

The client engaged Treeni to design and implement a comprehensive supplier sustainability program in three phases:

Phase 1: Supplier Segmentation & Risk Assessment

We helped categorize suppliers by spend and emissions intensity. High-spend, high-impact suppliers were prioritized for engagement.

↓

Phase 2: Data Collection Campaign

Using resustain SCSM, we launched a guided ESG questionnaire to 500 priority suppliers. The platform made it easy for suppliers to submit data, with built-in validation and support.

↓

Phase 3: Analysis & Ongoing Management

We helped the client analyze results, identify high-risk suppliers, and set up quarterly reviews to track improvements.
