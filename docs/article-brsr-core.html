BRSR Core is Here: Are You in the Top 250?

What changed in December 2024 and why your sustainability team should be worried (or prepared)

If you're a sustainability professional at one of India's top 250 listed companies, December 18, 2024, was a date that changed your workload forever. SEBI approved mandatory BRSR Core reporting with third-party assurance. Here's what that actually means for you.

What Just Happened?

For years, BRSR (Business Responsibility and Sustainability Reporting) was descriptive. You told stories about your sustainability initiatives. Numbers were helpful but not mandatory. Assurance was optional.

BRSR Core changes everything. Starting FY 2024-25, the top 250 listed entities must report on 9 key ESG performance indicators with mandatory third-party assurance. Think of it as moving from "tell us what you're doing" to "prove it with audited numbers."

The 9 KPIs You Can't Ignore

These aren't fluffy metrics. SEBI wants hard numbers:

  • Scope 1 and Scope 2 emissions (absolute and intensity)
  • Scope 3 emissions (at least 15 categories)
  • Water consumption and discharge
  • Air pollutant emissions
  • Waste generation and recycling rates
  • Employee diversity (gender, differently-abled)
  • Occupational health and safety (lost time injury frequency rate)
  • Board independence percentage
  • Anti-corruption training coverage

Notice what's missing? There's no wiggle room. No "we're working on it." You either have the data or you don't.

Why This Matters More Than You Think

1. Greenwashing is now legally risky. Third-party assurance means an auditor will verify your numbers. If your carbon accounting methodology is shaky, it will show. If you've been using spend-based emissions factors to inflate your "low emissions" claim, the auditor will ask questions.

2. Your data infrastructure will be tested. Can you actually produce Scope 3 emissions across 15 categories? Do you have supplier-specific data or are you relying on industry averages? Can you prove your water consumption numbers came from actual meters, not estimates?

3. Investors are watching. BRSR Core was designed to give investors credible ESG data. If your report shows poor performance or inconsistent data, it impacts your ESG ratings, which impacts your cost of capital.

The Scope 3 Problem Nobody Talks About

Most companies can handle Scope 1 and 2. You own the facilities, you have the electricity bills, you know your fuel consumption. Scope 3 is where it gets messy.

For an IT services company, 60-70% of emissions are Scope 3: employee commuting, business travel, data center cooling by third parties, hardware purchased from vendors. For a manufacturing company, it's raw materials, logistics, and end-of-life disposal.

Here's the uncomfortable truth: most companies don't have supplier-specific data. They're using spend-based emission factors ("we spent ₹10 crore on steel, so we multiply by an industry average factor"). That won't pass assurance scrutiny. Auditors will ask: "Did you actually engage your suppliers? Do you have primary data?"

What You Should Do Right Now

If you're in the top 250:

  1. Audit your current data. Can you produce all 9 KPIs today? If not, identify gaps.
  2. Start supplier engagement for Scope 3. Don't wait. Suppliers need 3-6 months to provide primary data.
  3. Choose an assurance provider early. Good auditors are limited. They'll be booked fast.
  4. Invest in a digital platform. Spreadsheets won't cut it for audited reporting. You need audit trails, version control, and automated calculations.
  5. Train your team. GHG Protocol, BRSR Core methodology, and data validation processes need to be understood across your sustainability, procurement, and operations teams.

If you're not in the top 250 (yet):

Don't relax. SEBI expands the mandate every year. In 2023, it was the top 1000 for basic BRSR. In 2025, it's the top 250 for BRSR Core. By 2027-2028, expect the top 500 or more to be covered. Starting now gives you a 2-year head start.

The Bigger Picture: Why SEBI Did This

India is aligning with global ESG standards: the EU's Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD), the International Sustainability Standards Board (ISSB), and the Task Force on Climate-Related Financial Disclosures (TCFD). Global investors want comparable, audited ESG data across markets.

By 2026, Indian companies competing for global capital will need ESG credentials that match European or US peers. BRSR Core is India's way of saying: "We're serious about sustainability, and we're going to prove it."

Final Thoughts

BRSR Core isn't a reporting exercise. It's a stress test for your sustainability infrastructure. Can you measure what matters? Can you prove your claims? Can you engage your value chain?

The companies that treat this as a compliance checkbox will struggle. The ones that see it as an opportunity to build robust data systems, credible metrics, and real decarbonization programs will thrive.
